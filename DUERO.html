"DUERO BOHOL"

Duero is a municipality located on the southeastern coast of Bohol, which is an island province in the Philippines. It is situated approximately 80 kilometers east of Tagbilaran City, the provincial capital of Bohol. Duero is known for its natural beauty and scenic coastal areas. It offers picturesque views of the sea, with charming beaches and cliffs dotting its shoreline. The municipality is surrounded by lush greenery and is home to several rivers and springs, adding to its natural allure. One of the notable attractions in Duero is the Can-Umantad Falls, which is considered the highest waterfall in Bohol. This stunning waterfall is nestled amidst a verdant forest and offers a refreshing swimming area at its base. Duero is also known for its rich cultural heritage. The municipality celebrates its annual fiesta in honor of Saint Nicolas de Tolentino, its patron saint. During this festive occasion, locals engage in various activities, including parades, street dancing, and religious processions. In terms of economic activities, Duero relies primarily on agriculture and fishing. The fertile lands in the municipality support the cultivation of crops such as rice, corn, and coconut. Fishing is also a significant livelihood for many residents, given the municipality's coastal location.
